# Three Dimensions of God's Presence

*Reflections from today's 5pm prayer service*

Have you ever wondered why some Christians seem to radiate Christ's presence while others struggle to experience God's reality in their daily lives? Why do some believers witness miracles regularly while others feel spiritually dry? The answer might lie in understanding the three distinct dimensions of God's presence that are available to every believer.

During our prayer service this evening at 5pm, we explored this profound truth that has the power to revolutionize how we experience God. It's not enough to simply know that God is with us — we need to understand **how** He is with us and what that means for our spiritual journey.

## The Foundation: Understanding God's Presence

Before we dive into the three aspects, let's establish something crucial: God's presence isn't just a theological concept or a feel-good idea. It's the very essence of what it means to be a Christian. When we accepted Christ, we didn't just receive forgiveness for our sins — we received God Himself. This reality forms the bedrock of our faith and the launching pad for an extraordinary life.

The tragedy is that many believers live beneath their spiritual privileges. They settle for a distant relationship with God when He desires intimate communion. They accept spiritual mediocrity when transformation is their birthright. Understanding these three dimensions of God's presence can change everything.

## The First Dimension: The Abiding Presence

> "On that day you will realize that I am in my Father, and you are in me, and I am in you." — John 14:20

The abiding presence is where it all begins. This is the foundational reality that occurs at the moment of salvation — God comes to dwell within us permanently. Your body literally becomes His temple. Think about that for a moment. The same God who created galaxies and parted seas has chosen to make His home within you.

This isn't just poetic language; it's spiritual fact. Paul reminds us in Philippians that the Christian life is essentially "Christ living in us and through us." We're not trying to live for God through our own strength and willpower. Instead, we're allowing the life of Christ within us to express itself through our personality, gifts, and circumstances.

The beauty of the abiding presence is its permanence. Unlike the Old Testament, where God's presence would come and go, Jesus promised that He would never leave us nor forsake us. This presence doesn't fluctuate based on our performance, emotions, or circumstances. It's the steady, unchanging reality that anchors our faith.

However, here's where many believers get stuck. They know intellectually that Christ lives within them, but they don't experience the reality of it. They live as if they're trying to reach God rather than recognizing that God has already reached them. The abiding presence is not something we achieve — it's something we acknowledge and yield to.

This dimension provides us with constant access to divine wisdom, strength, and peace. When we're anxious, we can draw from His peace. When we're confused, we can tap into His wisdom. When we're weak, we can access His strength. All of this is available because of the abiding presence.

## The Second Dimension: The Manifested Presence

> "Whoever has my commands and keeps them is the one who loves me. The one who loves me will be loved by my Father, and I too will love them and show myself to them." — John 14:21

While the abiding presence is about position, the manifested presence is about experience. This is where the theoretical becomes practical, where the invisible becomes visible in our daily lives. Jesus makes a profound promise here: those who love Him and obey His commands will experience Him manifesting Himself to them.

There's a thin but crucial line between Christ being in us and Christ being manifested through us. The abiding presence is a gift of grace — we don't earn it. But the manifested presence is activated through our obedience and love for God's Word.

Think of it this way: imagine you have a powerful generator in your basement, but you never connect it to your house's electrical system. The power is there, but it's not manifested in practical ways. Similarly, Christ's presence abides within every believer, but it's through obedience that this presence becomes manifest in our experience.

The manifested presence shows up in countless ways. It's the supernatural peace that floods your heart during a crisis. It's the wisdom that flows through you when counseling a friend. It's the love that enables you to forgive someone who has deeply hurt you. It's the joy that bubbles up even in difficult circumstances.

This dimension is where other people begin to notice something different about you. They can't quite put their finger on it, but there's a quality of life, a depth of character, a strength in adversity that points beyond human capability. This is Christ being manifested through your obedience and surrender.

## The Third Dimension: The Transforming Presence

> "Then Judas (not Judas Iscariot) said, 'But, Lord, why do you intend to show yourself to us and not to the world?' Jesus replied, 'Anyone who loves me will obey my teaching. My Father will love them, and we will come to them and make our home with them.'" — John 14:22-23

The transforming presence is where the miraculous becomes normal, where impossibilities bow to God's power, where we experience transformation from the inside out. This is the dimension where we become "one with Him in spirit" in the most profound way possible.

In this dimension, God doesn't just dwell within us or manifest through us — He transforms us. This is where the deepest work of sanctification takes place, where our inner being is renewed and reshaped to reflect Christ's nature. It's where miracles happen, not just around us, but within us.

The transforming presence is activated when we move beyond mere obedience to love-motivated surrender. Notice Jesus' progression in these verses: it begins with having and keeping His commands (obedience), moves to loving Him (relationship), and culminates in the Father and Son making their home with us (transformation).

This is where we need to shift our expectations. Every time we come before God — whether in prayer, worship, or simply acknowledging His presence — we should expect transformation. Not just emotional feelings or spiritual highs, but genuine, lasting change from the inside out.

The transforming presence is where generational patterns are broken, where deep-rooted fears are uprooted, where our character is refined like gold in fire. It's where we experience what Paul describes as being "transformed by the renewing of our minds." This isn't self-improvement; it's divine reconstruction.

## Living in All Three Dimensions

Here's the beautiful truth: these three dimensions aren't exclusive of each other. They're meant to work together in the life of every believer. The abiding presence provides the foundation, the manifested presence brings practical experience, and the transforming presence creates lasting change.

Many Christians live primarily in the first dimension — they know Christ is in them but rarely experience His manifest presence or transforming power. Others might have occasional experiences of the manifested presence but lack the consistency that comes from understanding the abiding presence. And some might have powerful transformation experiences but struggle to maintain them without grasping the full picture.

The goal is to live consciously in all three dimensions. To wake up each morning aware of Christ's abiding presence within you. To walk through your day expecting His presence to manifest through your obedience and love. And to regularly position yourself before God with the expectation of transformation.

## Practical Steps Forward

As we concluded our prayer service today, the question became: how do we move from understanding these truths to experiencing them? Here are some practical steps:

**For the Abiding Presence:**
- Spend time daily acknowledging Christ's presence within you. Thank Him for making His home in you. Practice "practicing the presence of God" throughout your day.

**For the Manifested Presence:**
- Commit to loving obedience to God's Word. Look for opportunities to let Christ's character manifest through your responses to daily situations.

**For the Transforming Presence:**
- Come before God regularly with expectation. Don't just pray for things to change around you; pray for God to change things within you. Expect transformation every time you encounter Him.

## The Invitation

The same God who spoke the universe into existence desires to make His home within you. The same power that raised Christ from the dead is available to transform your life from the inside out. The same presence that filled the temple in Solomon's day wants to fill the temple of your heart.

This isn't just theology — it's your inheritance as a child of God. These three dimensions of God's presence aren't for spiritual giants or super-saints. They're for every believer who desires to experience the fullness of what Christ has provided.

As we left our prayer gathering tonight, we carried with us not just information, but an invitation — an invitation to experience God's presence in ways we may never have imagined possible. The veil has been torn, the way has been opened, and the presence of God awaits.

The question isn't whether God is willing to reveal Himself to you. The question is: **are you ready to experience Him in the fullness of His abiding, manifested, and transforming presence?**

Your journey into the deeper dimensions of God's presence begins now.
